Why Prague?

Prague, the City of a Hundred Spires

Looking at the Czech capital form the gates of Prague Castle located high above the city, one cannot be surprised by the nickname "Prague the city of a hundred spires". Although the actual number of spires and towers in Prague is ranging from 500 to over 1,000. It was most likely the obscure Austrian historian Josef Hormayr who first penned the phrase, in the early 19th century. It was also around that time that mathematician and philosopher Bernard Bolzano counted Prague’s towers and gave it the nickname.

Even though Prague has other nicknames such as "matka měst" – "mother of all cities", "zlatá Praha" – "golden Prague", "srdce Evropy" - "the heart of Europe", "kamenný sen" - "a stone dream" or "perla měst" - "the pearl of cities", the 100-spired nickname is used the most. Therefore when you visit Prague you should try to see and visit as many towers as you can. The most popular ones are easy to find.
